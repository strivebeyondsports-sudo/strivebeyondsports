Our Mission

Bringing sport psychology to Illinois high schools that need it most.

Strive Beyond Sports is a policy initiative working to place trained sport psychology professionals inside Illinois high schools through a targeted grant pilot program — starting with the communities carrying the greatest need.

The Problem

Why student-athlete mental health can't wait.

Student-athletes face a unique intersection of academic pressure, physical demands, team dynamics, and identity tied to performance. General school counselors are not trained for this. The gap is real — and measurable.

01

Performance Is Directly Tied to Mental Health

Anxiety, depression, and burnout don't stay off the field. Research consistently shows that unaddressed mental health challenges reduce athletic performance, increase injury risk, and shorten athletic careers — often during the most critical developmental years.

Source: Journal of Athletic Training, 2021
02

Athletes Are Less Likely to Seek Help

The culture of sport — where toughness is celebrated and vulnerability is stigmatized — creates barriers unique to athletes. A sport psychology professional embedded in the school speaks that language, making it safe to seek support before a crisis occurs.

Source: British Journal of Sports Medicine, 2022
03

Illinois Is Already Acting — Athletes Are Left Out

In 2025, Illinois became the first state to mandate universal mental health screenings for all students (SB1560). But the implementation guidance does not identify student-athletes as a population with distinct needs. Strive Beyond Sports aims to close that gap.

Source: Illinois SB1560, signed July 31, 2025
04

High-Need Schools Are Most Vulnerable

Schools carrying the heaviest academic and socioeconomic challenges — designated "Comprehensive" by ISBE — also have the fewest mental health resources. Student-athletes in these communities compete with the least support of any group in the state.

Source: Illinois Report Card, ISBE 2025
05

The Return on Investment Is Clear

Schools that have implemented sport psychology programs report reductions in athlete dropout rates, decreases in sport-related anxiety presenting in the classroom, and stronger relationships between coaches, athletes, and school support staff.

Source: AASP Applied Sport Psychology Research, 2023
06

Illinois Has the Infrastructure — Not the Specialists

Illinois trains qualified sport psychology professionals at multiple universities. Certified Mental Performance Consultants (CMPCs) exist in the state. What's missing is the policy and funding mechanism to get them into schools where athletes need them.

Source: Association for Applied Sport Psychology (AASP)

The Plan

A Grant Pilot Program: Targeted. Evidence-Based. Scalable.

Rather than mandate a statewide hire overnight, Strive Beyond Sports is pursuing a grant pilot program to place sport psychology professionals in Illinois high schools designated as high-need by ISBE. The pilot builds the data record needed to expand statewide.

Phase One

Target the Right Schools

Pilot schools will be drawn from ISBE's "Comprehensive" designation list — the highest-need schools in Illinois. These schools already receive additional state resources. Sport psychology support completes the picture for their student-athletes.

Phase Two

Place Qualified Professionals

Grant funding covers placement of Certified Mental Performance Consultants (CMPCs) or master's-prepared sport psychology professionals in each pilot school — part-time initially, building to full-time based on athlete population and demonstrated need.

Phase Three

Measure, Document, Report

Pilot schools participate in a structured outcomes-tracking protocol: athlete mental health screening results, performance indicators, coach feedback, injury rates, and student engagement. This data becomes the evidence base for statewide legislation.

Phase Four

Scale Through Legislation

Armed with real Illinois data, Strive Beyond Sports will introduce legislation in the 105th General Assembly (2027) to expand the program statewide — with funding mechanisms, credentialing standards, and implementation guidance already tested.

The Road Ahead

A Realistic Timeline to Change.

Legislative change takes time. Here's an honest look at where we are and where we're going — built around the Illinois General Assembly calendar and ISBE's own implementation schedule.

Now — Summer 2026
Groundwork & ISBE Stakeholder Engagement

Submitting formal public comment to ISBE as they draft SB1560 implementation guidance (due September 2026). Building coalition of legislators, professional organizations, and school partners. Identifying pilot school candidates from ISBE's Comprehensive designation list.

Fall 2026
Legislator Meetings & Coalition Building

Meeting with SB1560 sponsors (Sen. Feigenholtz, Sen. Fine, Rep. LaPointe) during fall recess. Securing support letters from AASP, Illinois High School Association, and Illinois Counseling Association. Developing formal grant pilot bill language with a legislative attorney.

January 2027
Grant Pilot Bill Introduction

105th Illinois General Assembly convenes. Legislative sponsor files the Strive Beyond Sports grant pilot bill. Bill is referred to the Senate or House Education Committee for hearing.

Spring 2027
Committee Testimony & Floor Vote

Public testimony from sport psychology professionals, coaches, student-athletes, and school administrators. Coalition advocacy in Springfield. Bill advances to full floor vote in both chambers.

Summer 2027
Governor's Signature

Grant pilot bill signed into law. ISBE begins identifying and funding pilot school placements for the 2027–28 school year — the same year universal mental health screenings begin statewide.

2028–2029
Pilot Data → Statewide Legislation

Two-year pilot produces outcome data. Strive Beyond Sports returns to Springfield with evidence-backed legislation requiring sport psychology access in all Illinois high schools with athletic programs.

About the Founder
