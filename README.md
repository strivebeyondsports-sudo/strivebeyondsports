# Strive Beyond Sports

**A policy initiative to bring sport psychology professionals to Illinois high schools.**

This is the official website for Strive Beyond Sports, a grassroots advocacy initiative working to place trained sport psychology professionals inside Illinois public high schools through a targeted state grant pilot program — starting with the communities that need it most.

## About the Initiative

Over 200,000 student-athletes compete in Illinois high schools. Fewer than 1% of those schools have access to a sport psychology professional. Meanwhile, research shows that 1 in 3 student-athletes will experience a mental health challenge significant enough to impact their performance — and most will never receive sport-specific support.

Strive Beyond Sports is working to change that through:

- **ISBE stakeholder engagement** — advocating for student-athletes to be recognized as a distinct population in the implementation guidance for Illinois SB1560 (the landmark 2025 law mandating universal mental health screenings in schools)
- **Legislative coalition building** — partnering with Illinois lawmakers, sport psychology organizations, and school associations to develop a grant pilot bill for introduction in the 105th General Assembly (January 2027)
- **A targeted pilot program** — placing Certified Mental Performance Consultants (CMPCs) and master's-prepared sport psychology professionals in ISBE-designated high-need ("Comprehensive") schools for the 2027–28 school year
- **A data-backed path to statewide policy** — using pilot outcomes to support permanent legislation requiring sport psychology access in all Illinois high schools with athletic programs

## About the Founder

Founded by a sport psychology professional based in Country Club Hills, Illinois, holding a Master of Science in Exercise Psychology from Lock Haven University. This initiative is driven by firsthand knowledge of the gap between what Illinois student-athletes need and what their schools currently provide.
